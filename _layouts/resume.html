Hello world! I am a SAC Postdoctoral Fellow at the Stellar Astrophysics Centre in Denmark and a Visiting Fellow at UNSW Sydney in Australia. My research revolves around the study of variable stars, which are stars that change in brightness over time, especially due to pulsation. These stars are fascinating because they are key to all kinds of interesting astrophysical endeavours, such as testing our understanding of fundamental physics, finding the properties of exoplanets, uncovering the history of the Milky Way, and measuring the size and age of the Universe.

My Ph.D. research was carried out at the Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research and the Department of Astronomy at Yale University. I have also done research at several other national and international laboratories, including NASA, NIST, and NII. As an undergraduate student, I spent two summers doing research in Brazil through the NSF-funded SUNY Oswego Global Laboratory. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics as well as Bachelor's, Master's, and doctorate degrees in Computer Science. I was a Presidential Scholar at SUNY Oswego and an NPSC Graduate Fellow at Indiana University and Yale University.

I enjoy teaching and have assisted in teaching and supervising students both nationally and internationally from the high school level up through the graduate level. In my free time, I like playing the guitar, bass, drums, and other instruments, as well as composing and recording music.
